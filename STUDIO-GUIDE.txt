 BRICK RUSH — STUDIO PANEL & APPLICATIONS GUIDE
 Last updated: 2026-06-29 (v6)
===============================================================================

WHAT'S NEW (quick list — details further down):
  - Applicants must be in the BRICK RUSH Roblox group before they can apply.
  - Developers get in-app NOTIFICATIONS (approved / changes / assigned / comment)
    and a "FIND WORK" list of claimable tasks across games.
  - "My tasks" now shows progress to your next rev-share %, a claim-slot meter,
    state groups (needs action / in review / approved), quick Submit, and a
    receipt of what each approved task paid.
  - Team Analytics is role-scoped: the owner sees everything (and can filter by
    craft); a discipline LEAD sees only their own craft's developers.
  - Admin panel: site-visitor stats (today / week / all-time), delete a single
    application or wipe all (with warnings), Pending queue sorted longest-waiting
    first, and a "Reviewed" tab where accepted/rejected applications collect.


This file explains, in plain language, how the two systems behind BRICK RUSH
work: the APPLICATIONS pipeline (how people join) and the STUDIO PANEL (how the
team gets work done after they join). Nothing here runs on your PC — everything
lives in the cloud (Supabase) so it keeps working whether your computer is on
or off. If the cloud keys are blank, the whole site quietly runs in a local
"demo mode" in your browser instead.

Key pages:
  - index.html / apply.html  -> public site + the application form
  - admin.html               -> review applications (owner + admins)
  - board.html               -> the Studio panel (the task system)


===============================================================================
 1. APPLICATIONS — how people join the studio
===============================================================================

WHO SEES WHAT
  - Anyone can open the site and apply.
  - Only the OWNER and people the owner promotes to ADMIN can open admin.html
    and review applications.

REQUIREMENTS TO APPLY
  - Log in with Discord (so we can DM the result).
  - Be a member of the BRICK RUSH Roblox group. The form checks this on the
    "Who are you?" step — if the entered Roblox account isn't in the group, it
    blocks continuing and shows a join link. (If Roblox's API is down, it lets
    them through rather than trapping real applicants.) Toggle in config.js
    (requireRobloxGroup / robloxGroupId).

THE FLOW
  1. A candidate logs in with Discord, picks a role (Scripter, Modeler &
     Animator, or UI/UX Designer), and fills out the form (apply.html).
  2. The application lands in admin.html as "Pending".
  3. You (owner/admin) review it and Accept or Reject. You can leave a note,
     star/rate it, and tag it.
  4. The candidate is notified of the result on Discord (a direct message from
     the bot), so they always know where they stand.
  5. BAN: if someone shouldn't apply again, you can ban their Discord identity.
     A banned person can't submit new applications.

FROM APPLICANT TO DEVELOPER
  - On an ACCEPTED application card there is a "+ Add to game" button.
  - Click it, pick the game, and the person is enrolled as a MEMBER of that
    game with the role they applied for already filled in.
  - That enrolment is what gives them access to the Studio panel (board.html).


===============================================================================
 2. STUDIO PANEL — the task system (board.html)
===============================================================================

THE SHAPE OF EVERYTHING
  Game  ->  Boards  ->  Columns  ->  Tasks (cards)

  - GAME      = one Roblox project. You can run as many games at once as you
                like. Each game tracks its own completion %.
  - BOARDS    = every game automatically gets four boards:
                  Overview, Scripting, Modeling & Animation, UI/UX.
  - COLUMNS   = every board has five columns:
                  Backlog -> To Do -> In Progress -> Review -> Done
  - TASKS     = the cards. A card has a title, description, assignee, priority,
                difficulty, a coin value, due date, labels, an attachment link,
                a checklist, and a comments thread.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2a. WHO CAN DO WHAT (roles)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

OWNER / ADMINS  (full control)
  - Create games, create and assign any task, set coins & difficulty,
    add members, appoint leads, approve work, see Team Analytics, read all
    feedback.

LEAD  ("Head of Scripting / Modeling / UI/UX")  — studio-wide, set per role
  - Can create, assign, edit, move, delete, and APPROVE tasks on THEIR
    discipline's boards across every game. Think of them as your second-in-
    command for that craft.

MEMBER  (a developer added to a game)
  - Sees the whole game's progress and the Overview board (READ-ONLY — the
    Overview tab is marked "view").
  - Only sees and works inside THEIR OWN discipline's board (a scripter sees
    Scripting, not Modeling or UI/UX).
  - Can pick up, work on, and submit tasks assigned to them.
  - CANNOT change a task's settings (title, coins, difficulty, priority) and
    CANNOT approve their own work. Those are owner/lead powers.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2b. CLAIMING WORK (self-serve)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Unassigned cards show a "Claim" button. A developer can claim a task to
    take it on themselves — no need to wait for an assignment.
  - To stop people hoarding work, claims are limited by difficulty. By default
    one person can hold, at the same time:
        3 EASY  +  2 MEDIUM  +  1 HARD     (open tasks only)
    Finishing or dropping one frees the slot. These numbers live in config.js
    (claimLimits) and can be changed any time.
  - Once a task is claimed, nobody else can take it. The card shows the
    assignee's profile picture so everyone can see who's on it.
  - Admins and leads can still ASSIGN work past these limits — the cap only
    applies to people claiming for themselves.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2c. THE WORK -> REVIEW -> APPROVE -> PAY FLOW  (this is the important part)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  1. A developer works the card across To Do and In Progress.
  2. When it's ready, they hit "Submit for review ->". The card moves to the
     Review column and is stamped as submitted.
  3. The OWNER or the discipline LEAD opens it and either:
        - "Approve"          -> the card moves to Done and the coins are
                                 awarded to the developer, OR
        - "Request changes"  -> the card goes back to In Progress, with an
                                 optional note left as a comment.
  4. Only an owner or lead can move a card into Done. If a developer tries to
     drag it there, it's blocked with a reminder to submit it for review
     instead. This is enforced in the database, not just the screen — it
     cannot be bypassed.

  So: coins are earned ONLY on APPROVED work. Nothing pays out until you (or a
  lead) sign off.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2d. COINS & REVENUE SHARE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Each task is worth some COINS (set on the card by owner/lead).
  - Coins on APPROVED tasks roll up toward each developer's revenue share.
  - Conversion rate: 50 coins ≈ 1% (set by "coinsPerPercent" in config.js).
  - Developers see their own totals at the top of "My tasks":
        Earned  /  In progress  /  ≈ Rev share %
  - You see everyone's totals in the "Coins" ledger (top-right of a game) and
    in Team Analytics.
  - NOTE: the panel TRACKS coins and shows the implied share. It does not move
    real money — actual payouts stay manual and in your control.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2e. THE RAIL (left sidebar) — what each button does
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - My tasks        Everything assigned to you, across every game, with your
                    earnings banner on top. The number badge = open tasks.
  - Team analytics  (owner only) The dashboard — see below.
  - Feedback        Leave a note/idea/issue/blocker for the studio. The badge
                    (owner only) counts unresolved feedback.
  - Games list      Switch between games. Each shows a live completion bar.
  - + New game      (owner only) Spin up a new project; its four boards and
                    five columns are created automatically.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2f. TEAM ANALYTICS  (owner only)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Open it from the rail. It shows, across all games:
  - Headline numbers: developers, how many were active this week, tasks
    approved, total coins awarded.
  - A per-developer table: tasks done, tasks open, coins earned, ≈ rev share,
    activity in the last 7 / 30 days, and when they were last active.
  - A live ACTIVITY FEED: who created, claimed, submitted, approved, or
    commented on what, and when. This is your "what's the team actually doing"
    log.
  - The feedback inbox, where you can mark items Resolved or reopen them.

  The activity log is written automatically by the database whenever work
  happens, so it's always accurate and can't be faked from a browser.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2g. FEEDBACK
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Any developer can open Feedback and post a Note, Idea, Issue, or Blocker —
    a quick way to say "this is blocking me" or "here's a thought".
  - Developers see their own feedback; the owner sees ALL of it (in the
    Feedback panel and in Team Analytics) and can resolve/reopen each item.


===============================================================================
 3. WHERE THE SETTINGS LIVE
===============================================================================
  Everything you'd tune is in  assets/js/config.js :
    - coinsPerPercent     coins-to-rev-share rate (default 50 = 1%)
    - claimLimits         how many tasks of each difficulty one person can hold
    - adminDiscordId      the owner's Discord ID (full control)
    - SUPABASE_URL / KEY  the cloud backend (blank = local demo mode)
    - DISCORD_WEBHOOK_URL  optional: post task updates to a Discord channel

  Promote someone to admin or appoint a discipline lead from inside the app
  (admin.html for admins; the "Manage" button on a game for members & leads).


===============================================================================
 4. QUICK ANSWERS
===============================================================================
  Q: Does any of this need my PC on?
  A: No. It's all in Supabase (cloud). Your PC is never a server.

  Q: A developer says they can't see a board.
  A: They only see their own discipline + a read-only Overview. Add them to the
     game with the right role (or the "All-rounder" role to see every board).

  Q: How does a developer get paid for a task?
  A: They submit it for review; you or the lead approve it; the coins are then
     awarded and counted toward their revenue share.

  Q: Someone left the team — how do I cut their access?
  A: Remove them from the game in "Manage". They lose access to that game's
     board immediately.
